# Northwind Robotics — workplace investigation procedure (CONFIDENTIAL)

This document is restricted to HR Business Partners, the Chief People Officer, and Legal. Investigation records are subject to attorney-client privilege when conducted under Legal's direction.

## Initiating an investigation

Triggers include:
- A formal complaint via the ethics hotline
- A direct report to HR or a manager regarding harassment, discrimination, or retaliation
- A safety or compliance incident requiring documentation
- A management referral arising from observed behaviour

The receiving HRBP opens a case in the HR investigations system within 1 business day. Cases are numbered `INV-YYYY-NNNN`.

## Confidentiality

Limit access strictly to those with a need to know. Do not discuss case details in shared channels (Slack, email DLs) or with the subject's manager unless the manager is directly involved.

The complainant is told that confidentiality will be preserved to the maximum extent possible, but cannot be absolute, since investigation may require interviewing relevant witnesses.

## Interviews

Interview order:
1. Complainant
2. Direct witnesses
3. Subject (last)

Two HR or legal staff present for each interview. One leads questioning, one takes notes. Recordings are not made except with explicit written consent and Legal sign-off.

## Findings and outcomes

Findings are categorised as:
- **Substantiated**: a preponderance of evidence supports the allegation
- **Unsubstantiated**: insufficient evidence either way
- **Unfounded**: evidence affirmatively refutes the allegation

Outcomes range from coaching, written warning, suspension, to termination. Termination decisions involving substantiated harassment or discrimination findings require sign-off from the Chief People Officer and the General Counsel.

## Records

Investigation records are retained for 7 years post-closure or per applicable jurisdiction, whichever is longer. Records are stored in the restricted HR vault, not in Workday.
