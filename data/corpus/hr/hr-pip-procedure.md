# Northwind Robotics — performance improvement plan procedure (CONFIDENTIAL)

This document is restricted to HR business partners and people managers. Do not share with the employee being managed except as part of the formal PIP delivery.

## When to initiate a PIP

A PIP should be considered when:
- Two consecutive quarters of below-expectations ratings, OR
- A specific serious incident (missed delivery, conduct issue, peer feedback) that warrants formal documentation, AND
- The manager has documented at least two prior coaching conversations on the same topic.

## Drafting the PIP

The PIP must include:
- Specific behaviours or outcomes that need to change
- Measurable success criteria
- A timeframe of 30, 60, or 90 days
- Weekly check-in cadence
- Explicit consequences if the PIP is not met (typically termination)

HR Business Partner reviews and signs off before the PIP is delivered. Legal review is required for PIPs involving any protected-class concerns.

## Delivery

Deliver the PIP in person (or video) with the HRBP present. Provide the written document at the meeting. Do not pre-share.

## Tracking

Log the PIP in Workday under the employee's record with restricted access. Weekly check-in notes go in the same record.

## Outcomes

If the employee meets the PIP criteria, document the close-out and continue normal performance management. If they do not, proceed to termination via the standard separation process. See the separation runbook for severance calculation; PIP-driven separations follow the same severance schedule as performance terminations.
